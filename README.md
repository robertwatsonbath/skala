# SKALA alarm kinda-sort-of-sound-alike?
I was watching YouTube and saw video by the Chornobyl Family about the SKALA alarm. This is entertainment/education only, not intended to be an accuarate reflection. 

This is a LTspice simulation circuit I put together to play with. The original used Soviet-era Ge PNP transistors. 
Rather than mess around creating models I just bodged it to use jellybean Si NPNs. You can download LTspice for free from Analog Devices.

The component values are rather different to those in the Chornobyl Family schematic, but we must remember germanium and silicon transistors
are rather different animals (Vbe etc). One of the (many!) nice things about LTspice is that you can save output waveforms as .wav
files meaning that you can listen to what comes out.

I added a very simple RC lowpass filter at the output to get rid of some of the harmonics which otherwise make youe ears bleed.

I was a teenage boy at the time of the accident, we'd just started to study nuclear radiation in our physics class. 
We did observe a small increase in background radiation in the UK at the time. It made it seem quite a lot more real and not just something
on the news. Almost 40 years later I'm still fascinated by the whole thing - the science, engineeering and the lived human experiences.

I'd suggest subscribing to the Chornobyl Family YouTube channel if, like me, you're also interested.
